          In the Laulettu Kalevala (The Sung Kalevala) audiobook, as the name
          suggests, the Karelian-Finnish epic Kalevala (1849) is sung in its
          entirety through song, using a variety of traditional folk melodies.


          It is essential that the Kalevala, compiled and created by Elias Lönnrot based on Karelian, Finnish, and Ingrian runosongs, is heard and sung specifically as traditional runosong, because that is what the epic really is. The Kalevala can be challenging to read, but through song it can be experienced in an entirely new way. Runo singing once came close to extinction, but it has been successfully revived in recent decades. Some of the key figures in this revival can be heard in this audiobook. One of the project's central aims is that, through the familiar epic, the listener may gain access to the vast world of Baltic-Finnic runosong and folk poetry.


          The first episode of the audiobook was released digitally on Kalevala Day, 28 February 2023. The second episode on Kalevala Day, 28 February 2026, and the third and last episode on Runosong Day, 25 April 2026.


          The audio book is available on YouTube (@laulettukalevala) and also on etno.net site.

          Piot \[Feast] depicts joyful celebrations based on texts from the
          Baltic-Finnic peoples. The work itself is a small celebration, a feast
          - while at the same time it is a grand celebration of the female
          voice. Dedicated to folk music, the versatile musician, Doctor of
          Music, Docent of Folk Music, and Artist Professor Ilona Korhonen has
          composed and arranged a female choir work that remains faithful to
          traditional song styles and heritage texts of the Baltic-Finnic
          region. The piece resonates with the bone-shaking power characteristic
          of the Baltic-Finnic vocal tradition.


          All singers in the ensemble are professional musicians and folk singers holding Master's and Doctoral degrees in music:  Hanni Autere, Juulia Björn, Charlotta Hagfors, Emilia Kallonen, Amanda Kauranne, Ilona Korhonen, Emmi Kuittinen, Pia Rask, Ulla Silvennoinen, Anni Tolvanen, Mira Törmälä


          Piot received its world premiere in June 2023 as a commissioned piece for Tampere Vocal Music Festival (Tampereen Sävel). In 2026, pihla folk will release the piece as an album.
